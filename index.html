Margin Notes

We read the brands you know.
Here is what we would change.

No case studies yet, so we will show you the work instead of claiming it. We take a brand everyone recognises, quote its own line back, and mark the one thing we would do differently. This is the job: judgement, in ink.

Airbnb Travel / Marketplace
Belong Anywhere
In the margin

A beautiful promise, aimed at the wrong moment. What people actually feel at checkout is not belonging, it is doubt. Will the room match the photos, will the host cancel, is this safe. Belonging is the reward. Trust is the barrier. The brand sells the reward and leaves the barrier to the reviews.

What we would do Put the proof of trust exactly where the fear is, not at the bottom of the page.

Notion Software
The all-in-one workspace
In the margin

All-in-one is a feature list wearing a headline. To a new user it reads as a blank page and a homework assignment. The real magic is the finished room someone else already built. Sell the empty toolbox and you ask the buyer to imagine the work.

What we would do Lead with the finished space, not the box of parts.

Oatly Consumer
It is like milk but made for humans
In the margin

The voice is the moat, and it is a good one. The risk is volume. When every surface is a wall of clever text, the cleverness becomes wallpaper and the one devastating line drowns. Wit compounds when it is rationed.

What we would do Cut the wall to one line. Let the white space do the bragging.

Rolex Luxury
A crown for every achievement
In the margin

Luxury whispers. The moment it explains itself, it shrinks. This line does the customer's bragging for them, which is the one job the customer wants to keep. The strongest heritage brands name the object and step back. The watch has earned the silence.

What we would do Say less. The object is the argument.
